How it works

This simulation uses the Monte Carlo method to estimate the value of π (pi).

We have a unit square (1×1) with a quarter circle of radius 1 inscribed within it. The area of the square is 1, and the area of the quarter circle is π/4.

By randomly placing points inside the square and counting how many fall inside the quarter circle, we can estimate π as:

π ≈ 4 × (points inside circle / total points)

Points are colored blue if they're inside the circle and red if they're outside. As more points are added, our estimation becomes more accurate.
